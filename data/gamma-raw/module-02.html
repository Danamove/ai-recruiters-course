The Anatomy of a Strong Prompt: R-C-T-F

Role

Who the model acts as. "You are an expert technical sourcer."

Context

The facts it may use. The job spec, the profile, your constraints.

Task

The one job to do. "Write a boolean search string."

Format

The exact shape of the answer. "Return only the string."

Two Habits That Separate Good from Great

Constrain the Truth

Add one line to every prompt that touches a real person:

"Do not invent any facts, use only what I gave you."

This is what protects you from hallucination. Keep it in every prompt.

Show One Example

Paste one good answer and the model copies the pattern fast.

A single example is worth a paragraph of instructions. Show, do not just tell.

Weak Prompt

"write an outreach message to a backend developer"

No role. No context. No constraints. No format.

Result: a generic, templated message that screams copy and paste. The candidate sees it immediately and ignores it.
