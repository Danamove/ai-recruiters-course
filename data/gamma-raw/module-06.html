AI Agents for Tech Recruiters and Headhunters

Module 06: Build 2, The Screening Agent

Narrated by Ada, your AI course host

The Build That Saves the Most Hours

Screening is where recruiters lose their evenings. The Screening Agent scores and ranks your whole shortlist, so the strongest candidates are already at the top.

You Still Decide

The agent does not replace your judgment. It applies it.

Faster

No more manually comparing every candidate one by one.

More Consistently

Every candidate is scored against the same criteria, every time.

The Shape: One AI Step Across Every Row

For each candidate, the AI scores fit against your must-haves and writes a verdict, score, match, and risk. Then sort by score. A ranked shortlist comes out.

Step 1: Define the Must-Haves Once

Write 3 to 6 Criteria

Focus on the things that genuinely decide fit. Not a wish list. The real requirements.

Be Honest About Must vs. Nice to Have

Mixing these two categories is the most common mistake. Keep them separate.

This Is Your Judgment

The agent applies your criteria. It does not invent them. The quality of the output depends on the quality of this step.

Step 2: The Scoring Step

Use your Fit Scorer prompt. For each candidate, the agent returns a fixed structure every single time.

Field

What It Contains

Verdict

Strong, Possible, or Weak

Score

A number out of 10

Must-Have Match

Which criteria are met

Top Risk

The single biggest concern

Truth Constraint: Missing data is always recorded as "not stated." The agent never invents information to fill a gap. This keeps scores honest and auditable.

Example candidates scored: Alex R., Sam T., Jordan K.

Reliability Tip: Same Format Every Time

Consistent output is what lets the workflow sort and rank. Inconsistent free text cannot be ranked.

Structured Output

Fixed fields. Fixed labels. The same shape for every candidate, every run.

Sortable Results

Because the format is consistent, scores can be sorted and compared automatically.

Dependable Workflow

This structured-output habit is exactly what made the Fit Analyzer dependable in earlier builds.

Step 3: Rank and Flag

Sort by score, highest first. Tag verdicts so the tiers are immediately visible.

Strong at the Top

The candidates worth your immediate attention are right there.

Weak at the Bottom

No time wasted. You can see at a glance who does not meet the bar.

Reasoning Visible

Every score comes with the logic behind it. Nothing is a black box.

Step 4: You Review, Always

The agent ranks. You decide. The score is a first pass, not a verdict on a person.

Spot-Check the Reasons

Do not just look at the scores. Read the reasoning behind them. A score without context is not enough.

Glance at Low Scores

Check a few low-scoring candidates for unfair "not stated" penalties. Missing data is not the same as a bad fit.

You Make the Call

The agent surfaces the signal. The final decision belongs to you, the recruiter.

A Word on Fairness

Score the skills, not the person.

Use Only Job-Relevant Criteria

Every criterion in your scoring prompt must connect directly to the role. If it does not affect job performance, it does not belong.

What to Never Include

  • A candidate's name
  • Age or date of birth
  • Background or origin
  • Any protected characteristic

Full treatment of fairness and bias is covered in Module 09. But the habit starts now, in every prompt you write.
