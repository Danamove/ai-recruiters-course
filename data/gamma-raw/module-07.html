The Rule That Does Not Move

It drafts. You review and send.

This build never sends on its own. A human is always the last step before a candidate is contacted.

Never auto-sends. The workflow creates a draft only. The send button stays with you.

Scales your personal touch. Not replaces it. Every message still carries your judgment and voice.

The Shape of the Workflow

The pipeline is simple by design. A ranked shortlist enters. For each candidate, the AI drafts a message in your voice. Each draft lands in a column. You review, edit, and send. Nothing moves forward without your approval.

Step 1: Capture Your Voice

A message in a voice that is not yours is still a template. Paste two or three real messages you are proud of into the Outreach Drafter as your tone reference. The model copies your rhythm.

Why real messages matter

The model learns your sentence length, your word choices, and your level of formality. Generic prompts produce generic output. Your examples produce your voice.

Two or three is enough

You do not need a large sample. Two strong examples give the model enough signal to match your rhythm and tone consistently across every draft.

Step 2: Feed It Real, Specific Context

Generic outreach could have been sent to anyone. Personal outreach could only have been sent to that person.

  • Use their current role and specific skill
  • Include the why-they-fit line
  • Only facts from their candidate row
  • No invented details, ever

Truth constraint: The agent uses only facts present in the candidate row. It does not invent details, fill gaps, or assume context.

Step 3: The Drafting Step

For each candidate on the shortlist, the agent produces a structured short message. Drafts fill a review column, ready for your eyes.

One specific opening hook

Tied to something real about Alex R., Sam T., or Jordan K. Not a generic opener.

One sentence on the role

Clear and direct. What the role is and why it is worth a conversation.

A low-pressure question

Invites a reply without demanding commitment. Easy to say yes to.

Under 90 words, no filler

No "Hope this finds you well." No padding. Every word earns its place.

Step 4: You Review, Edit, and Send

Your review checklist

  • Read each draft against the candidate profile
  • Fix the voice if it drifts from yours
  • Cut, do not add. Shorter is better.
  • Send from Gmail. The workflow creates a draft only.

The workflow can create a Gmail draft. It cannot auto-send. The human send stays human, always.
