The Trap: Do Not Make Everything an Agent

The most common beginner mistake is building a complex reasoning agent for a task that only needed five fixed steps.

It costs more, breaks more, and is harder to fix. One decision keeps you out of that trap.

Two Words, Defined Simply

Workflow

A fixed set of steps that runs the same way every time. No judgment. A recipe.

Agent

Software that uses AI to make a judgment when the input varies. It thinks, within limits.

The Real Answer: A Workflow with a Brain

Almost every automation is a workflow with one or two AI steps inside.

The Workflow Handles

  • Triggers
  • Moving data
  • Writing to sheets

The AI Step Handles

  • The one moment that needs judgment
  • Variable input
  • Reasoning within limits

Example: Your Screening Agent

Only the middle step needs a brain. Everything around it is plain workflow. The AI judgment is the one moment of intelligence in an otherwise fixed sequence.

The Decision Framework: Three Questions

Can I write the exact steps, and they never change?

Yes means workflow.

Does it need judgment, or does the input vary?

Yes means add an AI step.

Must the AI choose between many tools on its own?

Rarely. Only then a full agent.

The Cost of Complexity

Workflow

Fast, tiny cost, predictable, easy to debug.

Workflow with AI Step

Still cheap and clear. The professional sweet spot.

Full Autonomous Agent

Slow, pricier, least predictable, hardest to fix.

Default to simple. It is the professional choice.
