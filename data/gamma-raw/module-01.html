One Mental Model: A Very Advanced Autocomplete

The AI behind every agent you will build predicts what words come next, given what you wrote. That simple idea covers a huge slice of recruiting.

You write something

A job spec, a candidate profile, a search query.

AI predicts what comes next

The most sensible continuation of your text.

You get useful output

A summary, a draft, a score, a boolean string.

Predicting the Next Words Covers Your Job

Every core recruiting task is a text-in, text-out operation. The model does not need to understand recruiting. It just needs to predict well.

Summarize a Profile

Given a raw LinkedIn export, produce a clean one-paragraph summary.

Draft a Message

Build a Search String

Where AI Is Reliable Today

Text in, text out. These are the high-trust tasks.

Summarizing

Condense a long profile into a short, structured brief.

Rewriting and Drafting

Polish a job description or write an outreach message from scratch.

Extracting Fields

Pull structured data from messy, unformatted text.

Comparing and Scoring

Match a profile to criteria and return a structured score.

Transforming

Convert a job description into a boolean search string.

Where It Lies to You

Never trust AI for facts you did not give it.

It makes things up, confidently.

This is hallucination. The model produces plausible-sounding text that is simply wrong.

It does not know today.

It has a knowledge cutoff. Recent events, new companies, and current roles are invisible to it.

It is confident even when wrong.

There is no built-in uncertainty signal. Wrong answers look identical to correct ones.

It carries bias from its training text.

The data it learned from reflects historical patterns, including unfair ones.

Two Habits That Keep You Safe

Give It the Source

Paste the profile directly into the prompt. Never ask the model to recall a real person from memory. The model only knows what you give it in that moment.

Keep a Human on Facts and on Send

The model drafts and scores. You verify and send. No AI output goes to a candidate or client without a human check. This is non-negotiable.

The Task Map

Not every recruiting task belongs to an AI agent. Here is how to split them.

High trust: you gave the source. Summarize, score, draft. Your agents live here.

Low trust: stays with you. Finding facts, making final decisions, sending anything.

The rule is simple. If you supplied the text, the model can work with it. If the model has to go find something on its own, keep a human in the loop.

Your First Prompt: Role, Context, Task, Format

Give the model four things, plus one truth line.

Role

Tell the model who it is. "You are an expert tech recruiter."

Context

Paste the profile or job spec. Give it the source material.

Task

State exactly what you want. Summarize, score, draft, extract.

Format

Specify the output shape. Bullet list, table, one paragraph.

Truth line: Do not invent any facts. Only use the profile. Say "not stated" when something is missing.

The Prompt in Action

A dummy profile for Alex R. drops into the prompt. Here is what comes out.

Input: Alex R. Profile

Name: Alex R.
Title: Senior Backend Engineer
Years of experience: 7
Skills: Python, Go, Kubernetes
Education: Not stated
Location: Remote

Output: AI Summary

One-Line Summary

Senior backend engineer with 7 years in Python and Go, experienced in Kubernetes-based infrastructure.

Three Strengths

Backend depth. Cloud-native tooling. Multi-language fluency.

Seniority Estimate

Senior. Consistent with 7 years and scope of skills listed.

Open Question

Education: not stated. Team leadership experience: not stated.
